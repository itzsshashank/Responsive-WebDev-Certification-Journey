Dr. Nambi Narayanan

Nambi Narayanan is a renowned Indian space scientist who made significant contributions to India's space program. Here's a brief timeline of his life and career:

  • 1941: Born on December 12, 1941, in Nagercoil, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1966: Joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), which was then in its early stages.
  • 1970s: Worked on various projects at ISRO, including the development of satellite launch vehicles.
  • 1994: Nambi Narayanan's career took a significant turn when he was falsely accused of espionage and arrested by the Kerala Police. This led to a highly controversial and protracted legal battle.
  • 1998: The espionage charges against Nambi Narayanan were dropped by the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) due to lack of evidence. The case against him was widely criticized, and he was later awarded compensation for the wrongful arrest and harassment he endured.
  • Post-Retirement: After his retirement from ISRO, Nambi Narayanan continued to be involved in space-related activities, research, and consultancy work.
  • Recognition: He received several awards and honors for his contributions to India's space program and for his resilience during the espionage case, including the Padma Bhushan, one of India's highest civilian honors.

Nambi Narayanan's life and career are a testament to his dedication to India's space endeavors, and he remains an inspirational figure in the field of space science and technology.
